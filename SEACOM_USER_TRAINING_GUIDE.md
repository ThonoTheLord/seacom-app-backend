## 1. Understanding What Seacom Does

**Trainer Instructions:** "Before we start learning how to use the computer system, let me explain what our company does and why your job is so important. Take notes if you need to."

### What is Telecommunications?
Telecommunications means sending information from one place to another using electronic signals. Think of it like this:
- When you make a phone call, you're using telecommunications
- When you watch TV, you're using telecommunications
- When you use the internet, you're using telecommunications

Our company, Seacom, owns and operates a large network of equipment that makes all of this communication possible. We have:
- **Towers and antennas** that send and receive signals (like giant metal structures you see on hills)
- **Cables underground** that connect different parts of our network
- **Buildings full of equipment** that process and route all the communication signals
- **Power systems** that keep everything running 24 hours a day, 7 days a week

### What is Network Operations?
Network operations means making sure all of our telecommunications equipment is working properly. Imagine our network is like a huge highway system:
- Cars (information) need to travel from city to city (people's homes and businesses)
- The highways need to be clear and well-maintained
- If there's a pothole or accident, traffic gets slowed down
- Someone needs to watch the highways and fix problems quickly

That's what you do! You watch our "highways" (the network) and make sure information can flow smoothly.

### Why Do We Need This Computer System?
Without our computer system, we would have to:
- Use paper notebooks to track what work needs to be done
- Make phone calls back and forth to coordinate
- Drive around to check on equipment in person
- Hope that problems get reported correctly

With Seacom, everything is organized in one place:
- All work assignments are listed clearly
- Everyone can see what everyone else is doing
- Problems are reported instantly
- We can predict when equipment might fail before it happens

### Service Level Agreements (SLAs) - What This Means
Some of our customers pay extra for guaranteed service levels. This means:
- If they have an internet outage, we promise to fix it within 4 hours
- If their phone service goes down, we guarantee it will be back within 2 hours
- If we break these promises, we have to pay them money as a penalty

Your job includes making sure we meet these promises. If we're about to break a promise, the system will warn you so you can fix it in time.

## 2. Getting Started - Your First Login

**Trainer Instructions:** "Now we're going to learn how to start using the computer system. I'll show you each step on the big screen, and then you'll practice it yourself. Go slowly - there's no rush."

### Step 1: Opening Your Web Browser
A web browser is the program you use to look at websites on the internet.

**Trainer Instructions:** "Look at your computer screen. See the icons on your desktop? Find the one that looks like a globe or has the word 'Chrome' or 'Edge' or 'Firefox'. That's your web browser. Double-click it slowly."

- If you see Google Chrome (looks like a colorful circle), click that
- If you see Microsoft Edge (looks like a blue 'e'), click that
- If you see Firefox (looks like a fox wrapped around a globe), click that

**What to do if you can't find it:**
- Click the Windows button (looks like a window) in the bottom left
- Type "chrome" or "edge" or "firefox" and press Enter

### Step 2: Going to the Seacom Website
Once your browser opens, you need to type in the address of our system.

**Trainer Instructions:** "See the bar at the top of the browser that has 'https://' in it? That's called the address bar. Click in that bar and type this address exactly: [insert your company website address here]. Press Enter on your keyboard."

The address bar is usually at the top of the screen. It might say something like "Search Google or type a URL" - that's where you type website addresses.

### Step 3: Entering Your Login Information
You'll see a login screen with boxes to fill in.

**Trainer Instructions:** "Look for the box that says 'Username' or 'Email'. Click in that box and type your username exactly as it was given to you. Don't add extra spaces."

- **Username:** This is usually your email address or a special code given to you by IT
- **Password:** This is the secret code you were told to create or were given

**Important Rules for Passwords:**
- Never share your password with anyone
- Don't write it down where others can see it
- If you forget it, ask IT for help - don't try to guess
- Change it every 90 days when the system asks you to

**Trainer Instructions:** "Now click in the password box. Type your password carefully. Notice that dots or asterisks appear instead of letters - that's to keep it secret."

### Step 4: Selecting Your Job Role
After typing username and password, you might see buttons to choose your role.

**Trainer Instructions:** "Look at the buttons on the screen. Click the one that matches your job title. If you're not sure, ask your supervisor."

- **NOC Operator** - if you watch monitors and coordinate work
- **Technician** - if you go out to fix equipment
- **Manager** - if you supervise other people
- **Administrator** - if you set up the system (usually IT people)

### Step 5: First Time Setup
The first time you log in, the system might ask for more information.

**Trainer Instructions:** "The system wants to know more about you. Fill in each box carefully."

- **Full Name:** Type your complete name as it appears on your ID
- **Phone Number:** Your work phone number
- **Emergency Contact:** Someone to call if there's an emergency
- **Location:** Where you usually work from

### Step 6: Setting Up Notifications
The system can send you alerts in different ways.

**Trainer Instructions:** "These are like alarms that tell you when something needs attention. Choose how you want to be notified."

- **Email alerts:** Messages sent to your email inbox
- **Phone notifications:** Text messages to your phone
- **Screen pop-ups:** Messages that appear on your computer screen
- **Sound alerts:** Beeps or chimes from your computer

**Trainer Instructions:** "For most jobs, choose 'Screen pop-ups' and 'Email alerts'. Technicians should also choose 'Phone notifications'."

### Step 7: Setting Up Location Tracking (For Technicians)
If you're a field technician, the system needs to know where you are.

**Trainer Instructions:** "If you go out to fix equipment, the system needs to know your location. Click 'Allow' when it asks for location permission."

- Click "Allow" when the browser asks for location access
- Make sure your phone GPS is turned on if you have a company phone
- The system will show your location on a map so others know where you are

### Step 8: Customizing Your Screen Layout
You can arrange the screen to fit how you work.

**Trainer Instructions:** "You can move things around on your screen to make it easier for you. Try dragging the boxes to different positions."

- **Drag and drop:** Click and hold, then move items around
- **Resize boxes:** Drag the corners to make boxes bigger or smaller
- **Hide/show sections:** Click the minus or plus buttons to show/hide parts

## 3. Understanding Your Job Role

**Trainer Instructions:** "Now let's talk about what you do every day. Your job is very important, and the system is designed to help you do it well."

### If You Are a NOC Operator (Network Operations Center)
You are like the air traffic controller of our network!

**What you do:**
- You sit at a computer and watch screens all day
- You make sure technicians are available when work needs to be done
- You handle emergency situations
- You make sure we keep our promises to customers

**Your main responsibilities:**
1. **Watch the technician list** - See who is available to work
2. **Check for problems** - Look for warnings about equipment failures
3. **Assign work** - Give tasks to the right technician
4. **Handle emergencies** - Contact managers when big problems happen
5. **Keep records** - Make sure everything is documented

**Trainer Instructions:** "As a NOC operator, you're the eyes and ears of the network. Without you watching, problems could go unnoticed for hours."

### If You Are a Field Technician
You are like a doctor who makes house calls for equipment!

**What you do:**
- You drive to different locations to fix equipment
- You install new equipment
- You perform regular maintenance
- You report what you find and what you fix

**Your main responsibilities:**
1. **Check your assignments** - See what work you need to do each day
2. **Travel to locations** - Drive safely to where the work is
3. **Fix problems** - Use your tools and training to repair equipment
4. **Take photos** - Document what you find and what you fix
5. **Report completion** - Tell the system when work is done

**Trainer Instructions:** "Technicians are our heroes on the ground. You fix problems that keep thousands of people connected."

### If You Are a Manager
You are like a coach leading a sports team!

**What you do:**
- You oversee the team and make sure everyone is working well
- You make decisions about how to use resources
- You review performance and find ways to improve
- You handle special requests and approvals

**Your main responsibilities:**
1. **Check team performance** - Look at how well everyone is doing
2. **Approve requests** - Say yes or no to special requests
3. **Monitor operations** - Stay aware of what's happening
4. **Plan for the future** - Decide what needs to be done next
5. **Handle escalations** - Get involved when problems are serious

**Trainer Instructions:** "Managers are the decision-makers. You balance what needs to be done with what we can actually do."

### If You Are an Administrator
You are like the mechanic who keeps the car running!

**What you do:**
- You set up user accounts for new people
- You configure how the system works
- You connect our system to other company systems
- You monitor the health of the computer system itself

**Your main responsibilities:**
1. **Manage users** - Add new people and set their permissions
2. **Configure settings** - Adjust how the system behaves
3. **Set up integrations** - Connect to other tools we use
4. **Monitor system health** - Make sure the computers are working
5. **Handle technical issues** - Fix problems with the system itself

**Trainer Instructions:** "Administrators keep the system running smoothly. Without you, no one could use the tools they need."

## 5. Daily Work Routines

**Trainer Instructions:** "Every day follows a pattern. Let's practice the steps you'll do each morning when you arrive at work."

### Starting Your Workday - Step by Step

**Step 1: Log In (5 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "First thing every morning: log in to the system. Do this before you do anything else."

1. Turn on your computer
2. Open your web browser
3. Go to the Seacom website
4. Type your username
5. Type your password
6. Click "Log In"
7. Wait for the main screen to load

**Step 2: Check Notifications (5 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Look at the bell icon in the top right. Click it to see if anything important happened overnight."

- Click the notification bell
- Read each notification carefully
- Click on urgent ones to see more details
- Mark them as read when you've handled them

**Step 3: Review Your Assignments (10 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Check what work is waiting for you today. Don't start other work until you know what you need to do."

- Click "Tasks" or "Work Orders" on the left menu
- Look at the list of items assigned to you
- Read the details of each task
- Note any deadlines or urgent items

**Step 4: Check Team Status (5 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "See who's working today and who might need help."

- Click "Technicians" on the left menu
- Look at the list of team members
- Note who is "Available", "Busy", or "Off Duty"
- Check if anyone needs assistance

**Step 5: Review System Health (5 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Make sure everything is running smoothly before you start your regular work."

- Look at the dashboard for any red or yellow indicators
- Check SLA compliance (service level agreements)
- Note any equipment that needs attention

### NOC Operator Daily Routine - Detailed Steps

**Trainer Instructions:** "NOC operators, this is your complete daily routine. Practice this until you can do it without looking at notes."

**Morning (8:00 AM - 9:00 AM):**
1. Log in and check overnight notifications
2. Review any SLA breaches that occurred overnight
3. Check which technicians are scheduled to work today
4. Review pending work orders and assign urgent ones

**Mid-Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM):**
1. Monitor technician locations and status every 15 minutes
2. Handle any new emergency calls that come in
3. Coordinate between technicians if someone needs help
4. Update work order status as technicians report progress

**Lunch Break (12:00 PM - 1:00 PM):**
1. Set "Away" status if you leave your desk
2. Check notifications one more time before leaving
3. Return promptly after lunch

**Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
1. Continue monitoring and coordinating
2. Handle end-of-day assignments
3. Prepare summary reports for management
4. Ensure all critical SLAs are still being met

**End of Day (4:30 PM - 5:00 PM):**
1. Assign any remaining work to tomorrow's technicians
2. Document any issues that occurred during the day
3. Log out properly

### Field Technician Daily Routine - Detailed Steps

**Trainer Instructions:** "Technicians, your day involves driving and physical work. Safety first - always follow traffic laws and use proper equipment."

**Morning Preparation (7:00 AM - 8:00 AM):**
1. Check your vehicle: gas, oil, tires, tools
2. Log in to the system from your phone or tablet
3. Review your assigned work orders for the day
4. Plan your route to visit locations efficiently
5. Load necessary tools and equipment

**Starting Work (8:00 AM - 8:30 AM):**
1. Update your status to "Available" in the system

**End of Day (4:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**

2. Update all remaining work orders
3. Report any tools or equipment that need repair

## 6. Handling Problems and Emergencies

**Trainer Instructions:** "Emergencies happen. Stay calm and follow these exact steps. Practice these procedures until they know them by heart."

### What is an Emergency?
An emergency is when:
- Customer service is completely down
- Public safety is at risk
- Equipment failure affects many people
- We risk breaking our SLA promises

**Trainer Instructions:** "Not every problem is an emergency. Use your judgment, but when in doubt, treat it as urgent."

### Emergency Response Steps - Follow Exactly

**Step 1: Acknowledge the Emergency (30 seconds)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "First, let everyone know you're handling it. Don't start fixing yet - just acknowledge."

- Click the red emergency notification
- Change your status to "Handling Emergency"
- Notify your supervisor if you're not in charge
- Document that you've seen the alert

**Step 2: Assess the Situation (2 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Gather information before you act. Ask these specific questions."

- How many customers are affected?
- What type of service is down? (phone, internet, TV)
- How long has it been down?
- What caused the problem?
- How quickly can it be fixed?

**Step 3: Notify the Right People (1 minute)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Tell the right people immediately. Don't wait."

- NOC Operator: Notify all available technicians
- Technician: Call your supervisor
- Manager: Call senior management
- Administrator: Notify IT if it's a system problem

**Step 4: Start the Response (5 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Begin fixing the problem using these priorities."

Priority 1: Public safety issues
Priority 2: Large numbers of customers affected
Priority 3: Critical business customers
Priority 4: Regular customers

**Step 5: Coordinate Resources (Ongoing)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Get help from others. Don't try to do everything yourself."

- Assign technicians to the problem
- Request additional equipment if needed
- Contact vendors for parts
- Keep everyone updated on progress

**Step 6: Communicate with Customers (Every 30 minutes)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Keep customers informed. They get more upset when they don't know what's happening."

- Send initial notification: "We know about the problem"
- Send progress updates: "We're working on it"
- Send resolution notice: "Problem is fixed"
- Always be honest about timelines

**Step 7: Document Everything (After resolution)**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Write down what happened so we can learn from it."

- What caused the problem?
- What steps were taken to fix it?
- How long did it take?
- What can we do to prevent it next time?

### Common Emergency Types

**Power Failure:**
- Check backup generators
- Prioritize critical sites
- Notify electric company if needed
- Monitor battery backup status

**Cable Cut:**
- Identify the break location
- Coordinate with construction companies
- Set up temporary service if possible
- Plan permanent repair

**Equipment Failure:**
- Identify the failed component
- Check if spares are available
- Schedule repair or replacement
- Monitor for additional failures

**Cyber Attack:**
- Isolate affected systems
- Contact cybersecurity team
- Don't click suspicious links
- Change all passwords

## 7. Looking at Reports and Charts

**Trainer Instructions:** "Reports show you how well we're doing. Let's learn what each chart means, step by step."

### Understanding Charts - Basic Concepts

**Trainer Instructions:** "Charts are pictures that show numbers. Think of them like a car's dashboard - they tell you how things are running."

- **Bar Charts:** Like a comb - compare different things
- **Line Charts:** Like a squiggly line - show changes over time
- **Pie Charts:** Like a pizza - show parts of a whole
- **Numbers:** Raw counts and percentages

### SLA Compliance Report

**Trainer Instructions:** "This shows if we're keeping our promises to customers. Green is good, red is bad."

- **Within SLA:** Jobs completed on time (green)
- **At Risk:** Jobs that might miss deadline (yellow)
- **Critical:** Jobs that will definitely miss deadline (red)
- **Breached:** Jobs that already missed deadline (red with exclamation)

**How to read it:**
- Look at the percentage at the top
- 95% or higher is excellent
- 90-94% needs attention
- Below 90% is a serious problem

### Technician Performance Report

**Trainer Instructions:** "This shows how well each technician is doing their job."

- **Performance Level:** How well they complete work (stars or percentage)
- **Workload Level:** How busy they are (low, medium, high)
- **SLA Compliance:** How often they meet deadlines
- **Tasks Completed:** Number of jobs finished

**How to use it:**
- Identify technicians who need training
- Balance workload between team members
- Recognize good performance
- Plan hiring needs

### Incident Response Report

**Trainer Instructions:** "This shows problems that occurred and how quickly we fixed them."

- **Response Time:** How long to acknowledge the problem
- **Resolution Time:** How long to fix the problem
- **Customer Impact:** How many people were affected
- **Root Cause:** What caused the problem

**How to use it:**
- Identify patterns in problems
- Improve response procedures
- Prevent future incidents
- Report to management

### Site Reliability Report

**Trainer Instructions:** "This shows how reliable each location is."

- **Uptime:** Percentage of time the site works
- **Downtime:** Percentage of time the site doesn't work
- **Mean Time Between Failures:** Average time between problems
- **Mean Time To Repair:** Average time to fix problems

**How to use it:**
- Identify sites that need more maintenance
- Plan equipment upgrades
- Allocate resources to problem areas
- Report to customers

- **Follow traffic laws** when driving

**Trainer Instructions:** "Do your work right the first time. It's faster and safer."

- **Read instructions** completely before starting
- **Take photos** before and after work
- **Test your work** to make sure it works
- **Clean up** when you're done

### Time Management Best Practices

**Trainer Instructions:** "Use your time wisely. Plan your day but be flexible."

- **Check assignments** first thing in the morning
- **Plan your route** to minimize driving time
- **Prioritize urgent work** over routine work
- **Take breaks** to stay alert
- **End your day** on time

## 9. Getting Help When You Need It

**Trainer Instructions:** "Everyone needs help sometimes. Here's how to get it quickly."

### Types of Help Available

**Trainer Instructions:** "Different problems need different types of help."

- **System Problems:** Computer not working, can't log in
- **Work Problems:** Don't understand assignment, need clarification
- **Equipment Problems:** Tools broken, parts missing
- **Emergency Problems:** Safety issues, urgent situations

### How to Get Help

**Step 1: Try Self-Help First**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Look for answers yourself before asking others."

- Check the help section in the system
- Look for similar problems in your notes
- Review this training guide
- Check your email for previous answers

**Step 2: Ask Your Team**
**Trainer Instructions:** "Your coworkers often have the quickest answers."

- NOC Operators: Ask other NOC operators
- Technicians: Ask other technicians
- Managers: Ask your supervisor
- Administrators: Ask IT support

**Step 3: Use Official Channels**
**Trainer Instructions:** "When self-help and team help aren't enough, use these official ways."

- **Help Desk Phone:** Call the IT help desk
- **Supervisor:** Ask your direct supervisor
- **Training Team:** Contact training@seacom.com
- **Emergency Line:** For safety or urgent problems

### What to Say When You Ask for Help

**Trainer Instructions:** "Explain your problem clearly. Give all the details."

- **What you're trying to do:** "I'm trying to log in to the system"
- **What happens instead:** "I get an error message"
- **What you've tried:** "I've checked my password and tried different browsers"
- **How urgent it is:** "This is blocking my work" or "I can wait a few minutes"

### Help Response Times

**Trainer Instructions:** "Different problems get different response times."

- **System down:** Response within 5 minutes
- **Can't log in:** Response within 15 minutes
- **Work questions:** Response within 1 hour
- **General questions:** Response within 4 hours
